1. Privacy-First Commitment & Local Data Sandboxing

SipUp operates strictly as a localized productivity and wellness assistant. We believe in absolute user data sovereignty. All logging histories, medication schedules, custom reminders, and daily progress trends are processed and stored locally on your device inside secure sandbox environments utilizing SQLite and LocalStorage structures [1.3.1]. We do not maintain, manage, or host any external servers to retrieve, upload, or process your personal health records [1.3.1]. Your raw data never leaves your physically owned device.

2. Android System Manifest Permissions Disclosure

To deliver advanced alert integrations, automated local backup systems, and real-time geographical climate target updates, SipUp requires specific system manifest permissions. Below is the full, mathematically and technically justified audit of our requested APIs [1.3.1]:

  • android.permission.ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION

    Grants access to high-accuracy satellite-based GPS coordinates. This coordinate parsing is executed strictly in the foreground. The coordinate data is transmitted securely to the open-source Open-Meteo meteorological API strictly to calculate local temperatures. This calculation determines the real-time hydration coefficient needed to adjust your daily intake target during intense local weather shifts. Location data is never stored locally or transmitted to private cloud servers [1.3.1].

  • android.permission.ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION

    Allows approximate network-based tracking coordinates when GPS satellite signals are obstructed, ensuring seamless fallbacks for the climate target adjustment system.

  • android.permission.POST_NOTIFICATIONS

    Required under Android 13+ rules to deliver high-priority system alerts, schedule reminders, and custom pill schedules to your notification shelf [1.3.1].

  • android.permission.SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM

    Bypasses Android system battery-saving standby cycles (Doze Mode). This ensures that custom medicine schedules and water tracking timers are executed precisely at the exact microsecond configured, eliminating background delays [1.3.1].

  • android.permission.RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED

    Listens to the operating system's startup completion broadcast. This permission is critical to re-initialize your local database-linked reminders and securely reschedule your alarms after your mobile device reboots.

  • android.permission.WAKE_LOCK

    Allows our explicit alarm receiver to briefly hold CPU processes awake. This prevents the OS from suspending active processes, ensuring that our localized custom Hindi/English voice packs play completely and clearly when an alarm fires.

  • android.permission.VIBRATE

    Allows the app to trigger localized physical haptic feedback when alerts fire, aiding accessibility when sounds are muted.

  • android.permission.INTERNET

    Grants network socket access to process localized temperature requests and sync storage configurations.

3. Advertisements, SDK Telemetry & AdMob Compliance

SipUp integrates the Google AdMob SDK to dynamically render non-intrusive advertisements (banners, interstitials, and rewarded videos) [1.3.1]. This model allows us to offer all features completely free of charge [1.3.1]. The integrated AdMob SDK may query, process, and transmit specific parameters to ensure safety and ad relevance [1.3.1]:

  • Device manufacturer details, operating system version, network carrier, screen dimensions, and locale configurations.
  • Anonymous mobile advertising identifiers (such as Google Play Advertising ID / AAID).
  • Diagnostics metrics, ad interaction timestamps, and click telemetry.

AdMob uses this anonymous profiling strictly to deliver region-relevant advertisements and protect against click fraud, strictly adhering to Google Family Policy and COPPA regulations [1.3.1].

4. California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) & GDPR Rights

Under the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), users hold absolute rights regarding their data. Since SipUp is designed with local storage sandboxing, your rights are fully guaranteed by design [1.3.1]:

  • Right to Access/Portability: You have full local access to your entire data history inside the app interface, and you can export it to your device's downloads folder as a standard CSV file at any time [1].
  • Right to Erasure (Delete Data): You can permanently purge all stored water logs, custom reminders, and streaks instantly using the "Reset Daily Intake" option in settings or by deleting the App's local storage via your system settings [1].

5. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) Compliance

SipUp is designed to fully comply with COPPA regulations. We do not knowingly collect, profile, or request personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13 [1.3.1]. All data remains localized within the on-device sandbox. Parents can securely purge all tracking parameters instantly by clearing the application storage [1.3.1].
